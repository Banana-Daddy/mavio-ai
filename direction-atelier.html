Origin

A founder, a 3D printer, and a living-room floor.

Mavio started in March 2025 as a one-person project. Sophie hacked together early prototypes, wrote the first computer-vision code, and 3D-printed brackets on her living room floor.

She'd just come off leading product at an AI + AR-glasses startup that became a unicorn — taking hardware from prototype to mass production with partners like Foxconn and Flex. Tennis was personal: a sport she loved, with no good answer for serious training between lessons.

Six months later, the team is small but exact: a hardware lead with twenty years of embedded systems, a redesigned launch wheel that survived field-test three, and the first integration between machine and AI app — under way as you read this.

How it works

Four moves, every session.

Built around how a coach actually works — observe, analyze, act, summarize. Mavio runs that loop continuously, every shot.

01
Observes

Computer vision watches your body, the ball, and the court.

Real-time tracking of stance, swing mechanics, footwork, and ball trajectory. No wearables, no extra cameras — the machine sees you and reads the rally.

Future shoot · live overlay
02
Analyzes

Patterns surface. Weaknesses get named. Strengths get logged.

Mavio doesn't just see — it understands. Stroke patterns, court positioning, contact-point consistency. A coach's eye, persistent across every session.

Future shoot · trajectory data
03
Acts

The machine adapts mid-rally. Speed, spin, placement.

Adaptive drills with progressive difficulty. If you nail ten down-the-line forehands, the next ball moves. The machine plays the game with you, not at you.

Real footage · field test 03
04
Summarizes

Every session builds on the last. A real practice arc.

Session statistics, progress reports, a development roadmap. Not vanity charts — a coherent path to actually getting better at tennis, week by week, year over year.
